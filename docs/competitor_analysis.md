# DraftGuru Competitor Analysis

_March 2026_

## Positioning

DraftGuru is an **aggregator**, not a content producer. We source the best insights from across the web — scouting reports, podcasts, video breakdowns — and layer clean data visualization, consensus analytics, and shareable tools on top. Sites like NBADraft.net, Babcock Hoops, and Draft Network are **sources**, not competitors.

## Direct Competitors

| Site | Est. Monthly Visits | Primary Draw | Threat Level |
|------|------|---------------|--------------|
| **Tankathon** | ~6M | Lottery simulator + live draft order | High |
| **Fanspo** | ~1-3M (growing fast) | Trade machine + mock draft sim + social | High |
| **CraftedNBA** | ~100-200K (est.) | Advanced analytics / 0-100 ratings | Low (closest to our lane) |
| **To The Mean** | small | Normalized consensus projections | Low (closest to our lane) |

### Tankathon
- **What they do well:** Lottery simulator is the killer feature — gamifies the tank race, drives repeat daily visits. Live-updating draft order after every game creates daily engagement. Run by one person (Matt Hoover). Multi-sport (NBA, NFL, NHL, MLB, WNBA).
- **What they lack:** Thin player profiles, no editorial/aggregated content, no social/share features, no combine data, no advanced analytics, no content feeds.
- **Traffic source:** Semrush, December 2024 (~6.29M monthly visits).

### Fanspo
- **What they do well:** Trade machine is the flagship. Social network layer (share/rate/comment on trades) creates viral loops. Mock draft simulator. Growing rapidly (110% MoM at one point). Multi-sport.
- **What they lack:** Shallow prospect evaluation, no analytics visualization, no content aggregation, community content quality varies.
- **Traffic source:** SimilarWeb, November 2024 (~855K-3.1M depending on period).

### CraftedNBA
- **What they do well:** Historical player comparison (any season since 1978), 0-100 prospect rating scale based on projected prime performance. Analytics-first approach.
- **What they lack:** No content aggregation, no share tools, limited UX polish, small audience.
- **Traffic source:** Estimate based on site characteristics.

### To The Mean
- **What they do well:** Normalized consensus projections from multiple draft models. Clean analytical approach.
- **What they lack:** Very small scale, limited features beyond the consensus model.
- **Traffic source:** Estimate based on site characteristics.

## DraftGuru's Edge

1. **Content aggregation hub** — No other draft site pulls news + podcasts + video into one player-centric feed with AI summaries.
2. **Share cards** — PNG export for social sharing. Nobody else does this for prospect data.
3. **Combine/anthro percentiles** — Most sites ignore combine data or show raw numbers. DraftGuru visualizes percentiles by cohort.
4. **KNN similarity comps** — Data-driven comps, not just "he reminds me of..." editorial takes.
5. **Retro-visual design** — Distinctive and memorable vs. generic sports-site aesthetic.

## Recommended Data Sources to Add

Priority order for maximum impact:

1. **College stats** (Barttorvik / Sports Reference) — richer player profiles, better percentile/comp models
2. **Consensus mock aggregation** (scrape public mocks) — powers mock draft + big board pages
3. **NBA standings** (NBA API / ESPN API) — enables draft order tracker
4. **Lottery probability rules** (pure logic, no external data) — enables lottery simulator
5. **Historical draft data** (Basketball Reference / Wikipedia) — archive pages + model training + SEO long-tail

---

## Bottom Line

DraftGuru's **content aggregation + analytics visualization + share cards** combination is unique. No competitor does all three. The two biggest gaps are **mock draft** and **big board** pages (universal table stakes). The path to ~400K/mo is achievable with those additions + social sharing strategy. The path to 1M+ requires an interactive daily-engagement tool (lottery simulator being the obvious candidate).
